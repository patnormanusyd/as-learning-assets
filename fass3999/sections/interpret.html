Categorising information

Information can be categorised in a number of different ways. You might be looking for company information, or maybe academic research, or maybe you’re just looking for something in the news. Depending what you’re looking for, you’re going to have to search in different places - and sometimes do you searching in different ways.

The table below that highlights the various forms information can take, and explains what certain sources are. When searching for information for your projects, it will be useful to know which types will provide the biggest impact and usability in answering and solving your questions.

Activity: With your research questions in mind, identify the relevant types of information that would be impactful in completing your project scope.

Where to turn for different types of information

While there's nothing wrong with using Google, it's worth considering what platform is the most appropriate for a particular content and source type. If you only use Google, you'll miss significant information that isn't freely available on the web that might better support your research.  Below is a directory of platforms according to different considerations.

Technological trends

Overview
How are different technologies used in particular industries? What is the latest technological development?
Source types
  • Whitepapers - analysis that presents the issuing organisation's perspective.
  • Conference papers - because technology develops so quickly, researchers often present and publish conference papers that capture developments, but don't require as in-depth research as a journal article would require
  • Journal articles - researched and referenced papers written by experts in a field.
  • Patents
  • Technology websites
Platforms
  • Gartner -  Provides research and insight into the current and future business of IT
  • ACM Digital Library - journal articles and conference papers published by the Association for Computer Machinery
  • IEEE Xplore - search a
  • IEEE's many journals journals and conference proceedings
  • Lens.org - global patent database
  • Google Scholar - journal articles and conference papers (multidisciplinary).  Set up full text via Library's link.
  • Google
  • Library Search - search across many of the Library's collections

Social, political and economic factors

Overview
What social responsibilities does a financial institution have? How will digital disruption impact a bank's social contract?  What are the risks economic and political risks of using new technologies?
Source types
  • Journal articles - researched and referenced papers written by experts in a field.
  • Scholarly books and book chapters
  • Government and NGO reports
  • Statistics
Platforms
  • Library Search - search across many of the Library's collections
  • Google Scholar - journal articles and conference papers (multidisciplinary).  Set up full text via Library's link
  • Subject databases - discipline-focussed resources, many of which allow you to search across journals in a field
  • Australian Bureau of Statistics - Central portal for a range of statistics on economic, social, population and environmental matters of importance to Australia

Regulation and legislation

Overview
What laws and regulation from government might inform or constrain a financial institution's actions?
Source types
  • Reports
  • Regulatory guides
  • Information sheets
  • Legislation
Platforms
  • Australian Government financial regulation -  Links to Australian financial regulators including the Australian Securities and Investments Commission
  • Library's law databases list (for case law, journals, legislation)
  • Federal register of legislation
  • NSW legislation

Competitive landscape

Overview
Who are ANZ's competitors?  What are they doing in the digital space?
Source types
  • White papers
  • News articles
  • Websites
Platforms
  • Google
  • Company websites
  • Company360 - Company information on 50,000 Australian public and private companies. 10 years of financial history & financial analysis, corporate family trees, director & executive profiles, shareholder information, the latest company news and developments.
  • IBISWorld -  Major provider of Australian and global industry market research, risk ratings, and Australian company research on Australia's largest 2,000 companies
  • Factiva - Newspapers, company information, pictures and media transcripts from Australian and overseas media sources.

Policy

Overview
What Government policy settings might impact the finance industry?
Source types
  • Government reports
  • Policy documents
Platforms
  • Australian Government Department of Finance policy and legislation website
  • Australian Government Department of Finance publications and reports
  • NSW Government Finance, Services and Innovation policies

News

Overview
What can news and current affairs tell us about finance and the economy, as well as how clients and customers perceive institutions, an industry and the future of banking and finance?
Source types
  • News articles and opinion pieces from both print and electronic news media
  • News articles in trade publications
Platforms
  • Factiva -  Aggregated news from major news outlets.  Search across publications.
  • Australian and New Zealand Newsstand - Aggregated news from major news outlets.  Search across publications.
  • Search the Library for individual publications
  • Google

User and stakeholder information

Overview
How can we understand what customers in different categories want?
Methods for understanding users and stakeholders
  • Surveys
  • Online ethnography - gleaning social media, review websites to understand users
  • Interviews
  • Personas
  • Focus groups
  • Market research reports
Platforms
  • IBISWorld -  Major provider of Australian and global industry market research, risk ratings, and Australian company research on Australia's largest 2,000 companies
